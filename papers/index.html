Failure of Averaging Marder Neuroscience 2002 People often think that the "true" value of parameters can be approximated by averaging over several realizations of a model, or experimental replicates. However, if your data is distributed in concave sets, then the mean of the set lies outside the set -- and is not representative of anything in the set.
